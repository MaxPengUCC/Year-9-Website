What Is Hunger?


The formal definition to hunger accoring to google is "a feeling of discomfort or weakness caused by lack of food, coupled with the desire to eat". Hunger affects 690 million people or an astounding 8.9% of the total population.

Where Does Hunger Stem From?

Hunger stems can stem from poverty resulting in not having enough expenses to spare for a healthy amount of food. This could be because a job doesn't pay enough to be able to provide you or your family food or because of a country's economy where there are not enough jobs in accordance to the people there, leaving many people unemployed and hungry.

Another factor could be education. A lack of education can hinder people from getting a good job which greatly cuts down on how much income they can have. This again ties into not having enough money because of low pay to get a healthy amount of food.

A third factor could be housing or renting costs. In many places, housing/renting costs are very high and can take a very hefty amount out of your paycheque. If you were to only have a minimum wage job and live in one of these places, it could be very hard to provide a healthy amount of food for yourself or your family.



The Hunger Cycle


Once people start experiencing hunger, there is a cycle that most inevitably fall in. Hunger can cause many health problems to all that are affected, this is emphasized on children as it can stunt their growth process. This can keep them out of schooling as they may be too sick to attend. The children might also have to leave school early due to having to provide for their families. This causes them to have an insufficient amount of education, limiting them to mostly minimum wage positions. This then repeats when said children have their own kid. They might not have enough to provide a sufficient amount of food to their children taking in the factors of their low pay, housing costs, transportation costs, etc... repeating the cycle. One of the only solutions for a family in this situation would be to seek food from a food bank. This is why donating to food banks can be so important, it helps families get out of the hunger cycle by making sure food isn't a worry for them. This can help them focus their money on other aspects of their life and can help keep their children in school because of that.
